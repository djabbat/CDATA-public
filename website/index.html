Core Theory

From Centriole to Senescence

The Centriolar Damage Accumulation Theory of Aging (CDATA), developed by Jaba Tkemaladze since 2005, proposes that aging is driven by the progressive oxidative modification of inductor complexes attached to centrioles — the only organelles that persist across cell generations without complete turnover.

Unlike telomere theory, which requires cell division, CDATA explains aging in both mitotic and post-mitotic tissues. Unlike mitochondrial theory, CDATA places centrioles at the causal origin — mitochondrial dysfunction is a downstream consequence of a collapsed centrosomal O₂ shield (2008).

Each centriole carries two distinct sets of inductor complexes: the M-set (mother centriole) and the D-set (daughter centriole). Reactive oxygen species — primarily mitochondrial superoxide — irreversibly detach inducers when the perinuclear O₂ barrier weakens. Cell potency is a direct function of the residual inductor counts (2010).

The mother centriole ages faster due to accumulated post-translational modifications (PTMs) that weaken inductor binding. This asymmetry is the molecular basis for asymmetric stem cell division and clonal dynamics (2025, 2026).

Centrosomal RNA plays a structural role in stabilizing inductor complexes. Its discovery and relevance to the aging cascade are described in 2012 and 2012 (RNA in centrosomes).

Beyond pool depletion, aging stem cells also divide progressively slower — a sixth, independent pathway of tissue failure. The molecular brakes (CEP164↓, spindle damage, ROS-quiescence, mTOR, PTM accumulation) are quantified in Tkemaladze 2024 and implemented as Track F in the Digital Twin.

Aging Tracks

Six Parallel Pathways to Frailty

Centriolar damage fans out into five mechanistically distinct tracks. Each is an independent Rust crate with its own ECS component and test suite, yet all converge on the central CentriolarDamageState.

TRACK A
Ciliary / Morphogen
CEP164↓ → cilia↓ → Shh/Wnt↓
→ regeneration_tempo↓
Primary cilia transduce morphogen signals. CEP164 loss collapses ciliation and shuts down niche self-renewal. Hill-function scaling (DT 2026).
TRACK B
Spindle / Pool Exhaustion
spindle_fidelity↓ → sym.div↑
→ stem_cell_pool↓
Spindle misassembly forces symmetric differentiating divisions, depleting the HSC niche. CHIP clonal drift emerges by year 40 (2023).
TRACK C
Telomere Attrition
Δlen = per_div × div_rate
× spindle_f × ros_f
Each division erodes telomeres proportional to spindle fidelity and ROS. Critical shortening triggers permanent G1 arrest via p21 — the Hayflick limit (2011).
TRACK D
Epigenetic Clock
methylation_age +=
dt × (1 + damage × 0.5)
Oxidative damage accelerates epigenetic drift. Division-coupled inheritance propagates the Horvath clock state to daughter cells. Calibrated to published epigenome aging data.
TRACK E
Mitochondrial Dysfunction
mtDNA↑ → ROS↑ → shield↓
→ O₂_at_centrosome↑
The MitochondrialModule models fusion/fission balance, mtDNA mutation accumulation, mitophagy flux, and the mito-shield — the primary O₂ barrier.
TRACK F
Division Rate Decline
division_rate = cilia_drive
× spindle_drive × age_factor
× ros_brake × mtor_brake
Stem cells divide progressively slower with age — independent of pool depletion (Track B). Five molecular brakes act in parallel:

1. CEP164↓ → cilia↓ → Wnt/Shh↓ → Cyclin D1↓ — morphogen-driven G1→S push is lost.
2. Spindle damage → p21↑ — G1 phase is prolonged; fewer divisions per unit time.
3. ROS-induced quiescence — high ROS stabilizes p16/p21, pushing cells into deep G0.
4. mTOR↑ (age-driven) — redirects resources from proliferation to damage repair.
5. PTM accumulation — impairs PCM remodeling before mitosis, lengthening G2/M.

The combined division_rate multiplies regeneration_tempo, so slow-dividing niches fail to replenish tissue even when the stem cell pool is intact. See Tkemaladze 2024.
Molecular Mechanisms

Eight Additional Layers (P13–P20)

Beyond the core inductor cascade, the platform models eight molecular mechanisms that modulate CDATA progression, each grounded in published biology and fully integrated into the ECS step loop.

P13
Morphogen Temporal Fields
Stage-dependent GLI/Shh/BMP/Wnt scaling via Hill function. Embryonic stages amplify signaling; elderly stages reduce GLI to 0.5×. References: Tkemaladze 2005.
gli = cilia^n / (K^n + cilia^n) × stage_scale
P14
Epigenetic Inheritance
Division-coupled methylation age propagation. Each division averages parent clock and chronological age — modeling Horvath clock inheritance in daughter cells.
meth_age = (meth_age + chron_age) / 2
P15
NK Immune Surveillance
NKG2D ligands (MICA/MICB) are expressed only under genuine ATM/ATR stress — not baseline ROS. NK activity declines post-40. Myeloid shift suppresses NK via TGF-β (DT 2026).
nkg2d = (ros×0.6 + agg×0.4 − 0.30).max(0)
P16
Proteostasis / Aggresome
Centrosome organizes aggresomes (Johnston 1998). CEP164 decay collapses aggresome routing; HSP70/90 oxidation reduces chaperone capacity. Combined: UPR stress rises.
aggresome_idx = cep164×0.6 + spindle×0.4
P17
Compensatory Proliferation
Pool < 50% triggers emergency ROS boost to drive proliferation — antagonistic pleiotropy: short-term rescue at the cost of accelerated long-term damage.
ros_boost = (0.5 − pool) × 0.30 if pool < 0.5
P18
Circadian Rhythm
CEP164 links ciliogenesis and the circadian oscillator (Clock/Bmal1 require cilia). Amplitude decay reduces proteasome night-boost — slowing aggregate clearance overnight.
amplitude = (cep164×0.6+(1−agg)×0.4)×(1−ros×0.2)
P19
Autophagy / mTOR
Age-driven mTOR upregulation suppresses autophagy flux. Caloric Restriction and NAD⁺ reduce mTOR → aggregate clearance independent of the proteasome. (Review 2026)
mTOR = 0.3 + age/100×0.5 − CR×0.30 − NAD⁺×0.20
P20
DNA Damage Response
Spindle failure → aneuploidy → DSBs → ATM → p53 → p21 → G1 arrest. Closes the full CDATA→cell-cycle loop via GeneExpressionState.p21_level (2026).
γH2AX = (1−spindle)^1.5×0.8 + ros×0.2
p21 += p53 × 0.3
Digital Twin Platform

Rust · ECS · 14 Crates

The platform is described in full in CDATA Computational Validation and Mechanistic (2026) and reviewed in Review of the CDATA Digital Twin (2026). The digital-twin approach to centrosomal dynamics is further contextualized in Digital Twins of Cell Differentiation Integrating Centrosomal Dynamics (2026).

human_development_example.rs 
// 1. Build simulation — register modules in order
let mut sim = SimulationManager::new(config);
sim.register_module(CentrioleModule::new())?;
sim.register_module(CellCycleModule::with_params(cc))?;
sim.register_module(MitochondrialModule::new())?;
sim.register_module(HumanDevelopmentModule::with_params(p))?;
sim.register_module(MyeloidShiftModule::new())?;

// 2. Run 100-year simulation (365 steps/year)
for year in 0..100 {
    for _ in 0..365 { sim.step()? }

    // ECS: query any component directly
    let mut q = world.query::<(
        &HumanDevelopmentComponent,
        &DDRState,
        &MyeloidShiftComponent,
    )>();
}

// 3. Add intervention dynamically
sim.add_intervention(Intervention {
    kind: InterventionKind::CaloricRestriction,
    start_year: 40, duration_years: 20,
});
⚙️
ECS Architecture (hecs)
Each stem cell niche = one ECS entity. Components are POD structs; systems iterate component tuples with zero-cost abstraction. No GC, no runtime overhead.
🔀
CHIP Clonal Drift
NichePool tracks 20 HSC niches with ClonalState. CHIP-like dominance emerges by year 40 from stochastic inductor loss — detected, not programmed. See 2023.
💊
8 Pharmacological Interventions
Senolytics, NAD⁺, Caloric Restriction, Antioxidants, TERT, CAFD Retainer/Releaser, Centrosome Transplant. Each with calibrated effect models. See D+Q 2019, D+Q 2022.
📊
Sensitivity Analysis
11-parameter tornado-chart. base_detach_probability dominates lifespan (×4.2 effect). CSV export for downstream analysis.
🌐
Multi-tissue Organism Model
OrganismState aggregates 5 tissue types. Systemic SASP, IGF-1/GH axis, inter-tissue ROS. Three death criteria: frailty / pancytopenia / neurodegeneration.
